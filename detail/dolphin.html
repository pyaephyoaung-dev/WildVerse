Bottlenose Dolphin

Tursiops truncatus

Bottlenose dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals found worldwide, known for their playful behaviour, complex communication, and remarkable problem-solving skills.

Scientific NameTursiops truncatus
Class / OrderMammalia, Cetacea
HabitatCoastal & Open Ocean
DietCarnivore — fish, squid, crustaceans
Weight150–650 kg
Size2–4 m length
SpeedUp to 35 km/h
Lifespan40–60 years
RangeWorldwide
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown, widespread

Interesting Facts

Dolphins sleep with one eye open, resting half their brain at a time.
Each dolphin has a unique 'signature whistle' like a personal name.
They can dive to 300 metres and hold their breath for 12 minutes.
Dolphins have been observed using sponges as tools to protect their snouts.
