Death's-head Hawkmoth

Acherontia atropos

Known for the skull-shaped pattern on its thorax, this moth is a strong flier and can emit a loud squeak when disturbed. It often invades beehives to feed on honey.

Scientific NameAcherontia atropos
Class / OrderInsecta, Lepidoptera
HabitatOpen countryside and gardens
DietLarvae feed on plants; adults feed on honey and nectar
Weight1-3 g
Size5–13 cm wingspan
SpeedFast flyer, up to 50 km/h
Lifespan2-4 weeks
RangeAfrica, Europe, and Middle East
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown
