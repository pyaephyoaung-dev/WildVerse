Queen Alexandra's Birdwing

Ornithoptera alexandrae

The Queen Alexandra's birdwing is the largest butterfly species in the world, native to Papua New Guinea rainforests. Endangered due to habitat loss and collection for trade.

Scientific NameOrnithoptera alexandrae
Class / OrderInsecta, Lepidoptera
HabitatLowland rainforests
DietLarvae feed on Aristolochia plants; adults feed on nectar
WeightUp to 12 g
SizeWingspan up to 28 cm
SpeedFlight capable; speed not well documented
Lifespan1-2 months (adult)
RangePapua New Guinea
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
PopulationEndangered; unknown exact numbers
