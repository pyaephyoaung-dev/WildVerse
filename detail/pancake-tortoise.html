Pancake Tortoise

Malacochersus tornieri

The Pancake Tortoise is known for its flat, flexible shell that helps it squeeze into rock crevices for protection. It is native to rocky outcrops in East Africa.

Scientific NameMalacochersus tornieri
Class / OrderReptilia, Testudines
HabitatRocky outcrops and savannas
DietHerbivore — grasses and succulents
Weight0.5-1.3 kg
Size15–20 cm carapace length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan30-40 years
RangeKenya and Tanzania
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationVulnerable; unknown exact numbers

Interesting Facts

Its flat shell allows it to hide in narrow rock crevices.
It can escape predators by wedging itself tightly.
Pancake Tortoises are herbivores.
They are threatened by habitat loss and pet trade.
