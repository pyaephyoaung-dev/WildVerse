Ball Python

Python regius

The Ball Python is a non-venomous snake native to West and Central Africa. Known for its docile nature, it curls into a tight ball when threatened, hence its name.

Scientific NamePython regius
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatGrasslands and open forests
DietCarnivore — small mammals, birds
Weight1-2.5 kg
Size90–120 cm body length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeWest and Central Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Ball Pythons are popular pets due to their calm temperament.
They primarily feed on small mammals and birds.
Females lay clutches of 3-11 eggs annually.
They use heat-sensing pits to detect warm-blooded prey.
