Tiger Snake

Notechis scutatus

The Tiger Snake is a highly venomous snake native to southern Australia. It has distinctive banding and is known for its potent neurotoxic venom.

Scientific NameNotechis scutatus
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatWetlands and grasslands
DietCarnivore — frogs, small mammals, birds
Weight1-3 kg
Size1.2–1.8 m total length
SpeedModerate; fast strike
Lifespan10-15 years
RangeSouthern Australia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Tiger Snakes are excellent swimmers.
Their venom contains neurotoxins and coagulants.
They feed on frogs, birds, and small mammals.
They give birth to live young.
