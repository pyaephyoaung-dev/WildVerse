Fossa

Cryptoprocta ferox

The fossa is a carnivorous mammal endemic to Madagascar, resembling a small cougar. It is the island's top predator, hunting lemurs and other animals. Fossas are agile climbers with retractable claws and a long tail for balance.

Scientific NameCryptoprocta ferox
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatRainforests and dry deciduous forests
DietCarnivore — lemurs, small mammals, birds
Weight5.5-8.6 kg
Size70–80 cm body length
Speed40 km/h
Lifespan12-15 years
RangeMadagascar
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Fossas have semi-retractable claws.
They can leap between trees up to 10 meters.
Fossas have flexible ankles that rotate for climbing.
They mate by hanging from trees, a unique behavior.
