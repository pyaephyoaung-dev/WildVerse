Iguanodon

Iguanodon bernissartensis

Iguanodon was one of the first dinosaurs scientifically described and was a large herbivore from the Early Cretaceous. It had distinctive thumb spikes that may have been used for defense or foraging.

Interesting Facts

Iguanodon is known for its thumb spike, possibly a weapon against predators.
It could walk both bipedally and quadrupedally.
Fossils were found in Belgium and England.
Lived approximately 125 million years ago.
