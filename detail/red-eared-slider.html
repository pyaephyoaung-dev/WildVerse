Red-eared Slider

Trachemys scripta elegans

The Red-eared Slider is a semi-aquatic turtle common in the pet trade and native to the southern United States. It is recognizable by the distinctive red stripe behind its eyes.

Scientific NameTrachemys scripta elegans
Class / OrderReptilia, Testudines
HabitatFreshwater ponds, lakes, and rivers
DietOmnivore — aquatic vegetation, insects, fish
Weight0.5-1.5 kg
Size20–30 cm carapace length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeSouthern United States
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationWidespread and abundant

Interesting Facts

They bask on logs and rocks to regulate body temperature.
Red-eared Sliders are omnivorous.
They have webbed feet for swimming.
This species can become invasive outside its native range.
