Numbat

Myrmecobius fasciatus

The numbat is a small marsupial native to Western Australia that specializes in eating termites. It has a long sticky tongue and unique striped markings. Numbats are diurnal and use termite mounds as their primary food source.

Scientific NameMyrmecobius fasciatus
Class / OrderMammalia, Dasyuromorphia
HabitatEucalyptus woodlands

Interesting Facts

Numbats have up to 50,000 termites in their daily diet.
They have no teeth as adults.
Numbats have a long sticky tongue up to 10 cm.
They are solitary and territorial animals.
