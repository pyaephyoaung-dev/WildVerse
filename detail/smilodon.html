Smilodon

Smilodon fatalis

Smilodon, commonly known as the saber-toothed cat, was a formidable predator of the Pleistocene in the Americas. It had long, curved canine teeth used to subdue large prey.

Scientific NameSmilodon fatalis
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatGrasslands and woodlands
DietCarnivore — large herbivores
Weight160-280 kg
SizeAbout 1.75 m body length
SpeedShort bursts, estimated up to 50 km/h
Lifespan8-10 years
RangeNorth and South America
Conservation StatusEX — Extinct
PopulationExtinct

Interesting Facts

Smilodon had robust forelimbs to grapple prey.
Its saber teeth could reach up to 20 cm in length.
Extinct around 10,000 years ago likely due to climate change and human activity.
Social behavior is debated but some evidence suggests group hunting.
