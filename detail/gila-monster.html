Gila Monster

Heloderma suspectum

The Gila Monster is one of the few venomous lizards, native to the southwestern United States and Mexico. It has a distinctive bead-like skin and a slow metabolism.

Scientific NameHeloderma suspectum
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatDeserts and scrublands
DietCarnivore — eggs, small mammals, birds
Weight0.5-1.4 kg
Size40–60 cm total length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeSouthwestern United States and Mexico
Conservation StatusNT — Near Threatened
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

It stores fat in its tail for energy during scarce periods.
Its venom is delivered through grooves in the teeth.
Gila Monsters are slow-moving and reclusive.
They primarily feed on eggs and small mammals.
