Thylacine

Thylacinus cynocephalus

The Tasmanian tiger was the largest carnivorous marsupial of modern times. Hunted to extinction, the last known individual died in captivity on 7 September 1936.

Interesting Facts

Despite being called a 'tiger,' it was a marsupial more related to kangaroos.
It could open its jaw to an extraordinary 120-degree angle.
It was hunted to extinction on mainland Australia 2,000 years ago.
Scientists are pursuing de-extinction using preserved thylacine DNA.
