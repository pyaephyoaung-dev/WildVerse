Chinese Giant Salamander

Andrias davidianus

The Chinese giant salamander is the largest amphibian in the world, reaching lengths up to 1.8 meters. It is critically endangered due to overharvesting and habitat degradation.

Scientific NameAndrias davidianus
Class / OrderAmphibia, Caudata
HabitatCold mountain streams and rivers
DietCarnivore — aquatic insects, fish, amphibians
Weight25-50 kg
SizeUp to 1.8 m length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan30-50 years
RangeCentral and southern China
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationCritically endangered, population declining
