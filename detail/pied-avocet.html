Pied Avocet

Recurvirostra avosetta

The Pied Avocet is a striking wader with black-and-white plumage and an upward-curved bill used for sweeping through shallow waters to catch invertebrates.

Scientific NameRecurvirostra avosetta
Class / OrderAves, Charadriiformes
HabitatSalt marshes, mudflats, and shallow lakes
DietCarnivore — aquatic invertebrates
Weight300-400 g
Size40–45 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan9-10 years

Interesting Facts

Avocets feed by sweeping their bill side to side in water.
They nest in colonies, often near water bodies.
Their long legs allow them to wade in deeper water.
They are known for their elegant and graceful flight.
