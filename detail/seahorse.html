Pygmy Seahorse

Hippocampus bargibanti

The pygmy seahorse measures just 2 cm. Masters of camouflage, they almost perfectly mimic the fan corals they live on, and were only discovered in 1969.

Scientific NameHippocampus bargibanti
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Syngnathiformes
HabitatCoral Reefs
DietCarnivore — small crustaceans and plankton
WeightUnder 1 g
SizeUp to 2.7 cm length
SpeedVery slow swimmer
LifespanUp to 5 years
RangeIndo-Pacific Ocean
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Male seahorses carry and give birth to the young — the only truly pregnant males in nature.
Scientists only noticed them after studying coral samples in a lab.
They pair for life and greet each other daily with a dance.
Their eyes move independently, like a chameleon.
