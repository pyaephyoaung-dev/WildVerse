Northern Flicker

Colaptes auratus

The Northern Flicker is a woodpecker species recognizable by its barred back and spotted belly. Unlike many woodpeckers, it often feeds on the ground, eating ants and beetles.

Interesting Facts

Northern Flickers drum on trees to communicate and establish territory.
They have a distinctive loud call known as a 'flicker call.'
There are two main color morphs: red-shafted and yellow-shafted.
They excavate nest cavities in dead trees or posts.
