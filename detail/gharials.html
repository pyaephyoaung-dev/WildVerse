Gharial

Gavialis gangeticus

Gharials are fish-eating crocodilians with long, slender snouts adapted for catching fish. Critically endangered, they are threatened by river pollution and habitat fragmentation.

Scientific NameGavialis gangeticus
Class / OrderReptilia, Crocodylia
HabitatFast-flowing rivers
DietCarnivore — primarily fish
Weight160-450 kg
Size3.5–6 m length
SpeedFast swimmer; up to 35 km/h
Lifespan40-60 years
RangeNorthern Indian subcontinent
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationCritically endangered; ~900 individuals

Interesting Facts

Distinctive long, narrow snout with bulbous growth in males.
Primarily piscivorous (fish-eating).
Populations declined due to sand mining and fishing nets.
One of the longest snouted crocodilians.
