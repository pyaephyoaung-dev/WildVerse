Greater Flamingo

Phoenicopterus roseus

Greater flamingos are the largest flamingo species, recognizable by their striking pink plumage. Their colour comes from carotenoid pigments in the algae and invertebrates they eat.

Scientific NamePhoenicopterus roseus
Class / OrderAves, Phoenicopteriformes
HabitatLagoons, Mudflats & Saltpans
DietOmnivore — algae, crustaceans, mollusks
Weight2–4 kg
Size110–150 cm height
SpeedUp to 60 km/h flying
Lifespan40 years
RangeAfrica, Asia, Europe
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~1.5 million

Interesting Facts

A flamingo's pink colour fades if it doesn't eat enough carotenoids.
They can fly up to 600 km in a single night when migrating.
Flamingos are more closely related to grebes than to storks.
They can live in highly caustic alkaline lakes that would burn human skin.
