Red Wolf

Canis rufus

The red wolf is a critically endangered canid native to the southeastern United States. It faces threats from habitat loss, hybridization, and human conflict.

Scientific NameCanis rufus
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatForests, swamps, and coastal prairies
DietCarnivore — small to medium mammals
Weight20-36 kg
Size90–120 cm body length
SpeedUp to 50 km/h
Lifespan6-8 years in wild
RangeSoutheastern USA
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
Population~20–30 in wild

Interesting Facts

Smaller and more slender than gray wolves.
Pack social structure with monogamous pairs.
Population reduced to fewer than 20 in wild at one point.
Captive breeding and reintroduction programs ongoing.
