Dusky Grouse

Dendragapus obscurus

The Dusky Grouse is a medium-sized game bird native to mountainous forests of western North America. Males perform elaborate displays during mating season to attract females.

Scientific NameDendragapus obscurus
Class / OrderAves, Galliformes
HabitatConiferous forests and alpine meadows
DietOmnivore — leaves, berries, insects
Weight800-1,200 g
Size45–60 cm body length
SpeedShort bursts of flight, up to 40 km/h
Lifespan3-5 years
RangeWestern United States and Canada
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Males have a distinctive swollen neck sac used in courtship.
They feed on pine needles, berries, and insects seasonally.
Dusky Grouse can fly short distances to escape predators.
They roost in trees at night for protection.
