Sumatran Tiger

Panthera tigris sumatrae

The Sumatran tiger is the smallest surviving tiger subspecies, with fewer than 400 remaining in the wild. Found only on Sumatra, they are threatened by deforestation and poaching.

Scientific NamePanthera tigris sumatrae
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatTropical Rainforest
DietCarnivore — deer, wild boar, and other mammals
Weight75–140 kg
Size2.4–2.75 m total length
SpeedUp to 60 km/h in short bursts
Lifespan15–20 years
RangeSumatra, Indonesia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~400–500 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Sumatran tigers have more closely spaced stripes than other tiger subspecies.
They are excellent swimmers and have been observed swimming between islands.
A single tiger requires a territory of up to 400 km² of forest.
Their habitat has shrunk by 70% in the last 25 years due to palm oil farming.
