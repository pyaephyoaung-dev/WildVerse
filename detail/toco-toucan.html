Toco Toucan

Ramphastos toco

The Toco Toucan is the largest and most recognizable toucan species with a massive orange bill. Native to South American tropical forests, it feeds on fruits and occasionally small animals.

Scientific NameRamphastos toco
Class / OrderAves, Piciformes
HabitatTropical and subtropical forests
DietOmnivore — fruits, insects, small reptiles
Weight500-860 g
Size55–65 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan15-20 years
RangeCentral and eastern South America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Their large bill helps regulate body temperature.
Toco Toucans nest in tree cavities.
They are social birds often found in small groups.
Their call is loud and croaking, useful in dense forest.
