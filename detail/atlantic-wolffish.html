Atlantic Wolffish

Anarhichas lupus

The Atlantic wolffish is a large, eel-like bottom dweller with powerful jaws and canine-like teeth, adapted to crush hard-shelled prey such as sea urchins and mollusks.

Scientific NameAnarhichas lupus
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Perciformes
HabitatCold coastal waters, rocky seabeds
DietCarnivore — mollusks, crustaceans, fish
WeightUp to 18 kg
SizeUp to 1.5 m length
SpeedSlow swimmer
Lifespan12-14 years
RangeNorth Atlantic Ocean
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationUnknown, declining

Interesting Facts

Has antifreeze proteins in its blood to survive icy waters.
Plays a key role in controlling sea urchin populations.
Lays egg clusters guarded by males.
Population decline due to overfishing and habitat loss.
