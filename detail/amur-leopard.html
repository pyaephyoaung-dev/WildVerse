Amur Leopard

Panthera pardus orientalis

The world's rarest wild cat, with only around 90 individuals in the wild. Adapted to cold, snowy climates of the Russian Far East, they are threatened by poaching and habitat loss.

Scientific NamePanthera pardus orientalis
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatTemperate Forests
DietCarnivore — deer, hares, and other mammals
Weight25–48 kg
Size1.2–1.5 m body length
SpeedUp to 60 km/h
Lifespan10–15 years
RangeRussian Far East
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~120–150 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Amur leopards can run at 60 km/h and leap over 5 metres horizontally.
They carry prey twice their body weight up into trees to protect it from bears.
Males sometimes help females raise cubs — unusual for solitary cats.
Camera traps allow scientists to identify individuals by their unique spot patterns.
