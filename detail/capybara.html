Capybara

Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris

The capybara is the largest living rodent, highly social and semi-aquatic. It lives in groups near water bodies and is an excellent swimmer. Its herbivorous diet consists mainly of grasses and aquatic plants.

Scientific NameHydrochoerus hydrochaeris
Class / OrderMammalia, Rodentia
HabitatWetlands and riverbanks
DietHerbivore — grasses and aquatic plants
Weight35-66 kg

Speed35 km/h

Interesting Facts

Capybaras have webbed feet for swimming.
They communicate with grunts and barks.
Their social groups can include up to 20 individuals.
Capybaras practice coprophagy to digest cellulose better.
