Zebra Swallowtail Butterfly

Eurytides marcellus

This butterfly is easily identified by its black and white striped wings and long tails. Caterpillars feed exclusively on pawpaw plants.

Scientific NameEurytides marcellus
Class / OrderInsecta, Lepidoptera
HabitatDeciduous woodlands and riverbanks
DietLarvae feed on pawpaw leaves; adults feed on nectar
Weight0.3-0.6 g
Size6–9 cm wingspan
SpeedModerate flyer
Lifespan2-3 weeks
RangeEastern North America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon

Interesting Facts

Has up to three broods per year in southern range.
Adults feed on nectar from various flowers.
Caterpillars have osmeterium to deter predators.
Tails mimic antennae to confuse predators.
