Chinese Giant Salamander

Andrias davidianus

The Chinese giant salamander is the world's largest amphibian, reaching up to 1.8 metres. Its lineage stretches back 170 million years, making it a true living fossil.

Scientific NameAndrias davidianus
Class / OrderAmphibia, Caudata
HabitatRocky Mountain Streams

WeightUp to 60 kg

SpeedSlow-moving

Interesting Facts

They are essentially blind and hunt by detecting vibrations through their skin.
They reproduce only once a year and males guard the egg mass.
Considered a delicacy in China, they have been hunted near to extinction.
Their lineage is older than the dinosaurs' extinction — 170 million years.
