Western Swamp Tortoise

Pseudemydura umbrina

The western swamp tortoise is one of Australia's rarest reptiles, adapted to seasonal wetlands. Critically endangered due to habitat loss and altered hydrology.

Scientific NamePseudemydura umbrina
Class / OrderReptilia, Testudines
HabitatSeasonal freshwater swamps
DietOmnivore — aquatic plants, insects
Weight0.8-1.5 kg
Size20–25 cm shell length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan40-50 years
RangeSouthwestern Australia
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
Population~250

Interesting Facts

Smallest tortoise species in Australia.
Spends dry season buried underground.
Conservation includes captive breeding and habitat restoration.
Population was once down to fewer than 30 individuals.
