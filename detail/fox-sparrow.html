Fox Sparrow

Passerella iliaca

The Fox Sparrow is a robust sparrow with reddish-brown plumage and heavily streaked underparts. It is known for its melodious song and secretive behavior in dense shrubbery.

Scientific NamePasserella iliaca
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatDense thickets and forest undergrowth
DietOmnivore — seeds, insects
Weight30-50 g
Size16–22 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan3-5 years
RangeNorth America, mainly northern and western forests
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Fox Sparrows migrate southward for winter.
They forage mostly on the ground for seeds and insects.
There are several subspecies with slight plumage variations.
Their song is rich and varied, useful for territory defense.
