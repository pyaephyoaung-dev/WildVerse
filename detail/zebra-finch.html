Zebra Finch

Taeniopygia guttata

The Zebra Finch is a small passerine bird native to Australia known for its distinctive black and white striped tail and orange cheek patches. It is a popular model organism in scientific research.

Scientific NameTaeniopygia guttata
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatGrasslands and open woodlands
DietGranivore — primarily grass seeds
Weight10-15 g
Size10–12 cm length
SpeedModerate flyer, exact speed not well documented
Lifespan5-9 years
RangeAustralia and Indonesia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationAbundant, widespread in captivity and wild

Interesting Facts

Zebra Finches are highly social and breed readily in captivity.
They sing complex songs learned from parents.
They are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
Their populations are stable despite habitat changes.
