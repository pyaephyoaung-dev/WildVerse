Yellow-bellied Sea Snake

Hydrophis platurus

The Yellow-bellied Sea Snake is a highly venomous, fully aquatic snake found in tropical ocean waters. It is one of the few sea snakes with a worldwide distribution.

Scientific NameHydrophis platurus
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatTropical marine waters
DietCarnivore — fish and eels
Weight1-3 kg
Size1.2–1.4 m total length
SpeedModerate swimmer
Lifespan10-15 years
RangeIndo-Pacific oceans worldwide
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

It can stay submerged for up to an hour.
Its venom is highly potent but it is generally non-aggressive.
It gives birth to live young at sea.
It has a flattened tail to aid swimming.
