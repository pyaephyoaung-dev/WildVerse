Pygmy Three-toed Sloth

Bradypus pygmaeus

The pygmy three-toed sloth is one of the smallest sloth species, restricted to a small mangrove island in Panama. Critically endangered due to its limited range and habitat degradation.

Scientific NameBradypus pygmaeus
Class / OrderMammalia, Pilosa
HabitatMangrove forests
DietHerbivore — primarily red mangrove leaves
Weight2.5-3.5 kg
Size40–50 cm length
SpeedVery slow-moving
Lifespan10-20 years
RangeIsla Escudo de Veraguas, Panama
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
Population~79 individuals
