Antelope

Antilopinae

Antelopes are a group of African ungulates belonging to the family Bovidae. They are known for their agility and speed, often using their keen senses to detect predators.

Scientific NameAntilopinae
Class / OrderMammalia, Artiodactyla
HabitatSavannas & Grasslands
DietHerbivore — grasses and leaves
Weight20–100 kg
SizeVaries widely; 1–1.5 m body length typical
SpeedUp to 80 km/h
Lifespan10–20 years
RangeAfrica
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationVaries by species, many stable

Interesting Facts

Antelopes are known for their agility and speed.
They have excellent hearing and vision.
Antelopes can run up to 70 km/h in short bursts.
A single antelope can consume up to 10 kg of vegetation in one day.
