Red Panda

Ailurus fulgens

The red panda is a small arboreal mammal with reddish-brown fur and a bushy ringed tail. It primarily feeds on bamboo but is classified as a carnivore. Native to the Himalayas, it is solitary and active mainly at dawn and dusk.

Scientific NameAilurus fulgens
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatTemperate forests with bamboo
DietHerbivore — bamboo, fruits, eggs
Weight3-6 kg
Size50–64 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan8-10 years
RangeEastern Himalayas and southwestern China
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~10,000 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Red pandas have a false thumb to help grasp bamboo.
They mark territory with scent glands on their feet.
Red pandas are excellent climbers and spend most time in trees.
They communicate with whistles and squeals.
