Eastern Bluebird

Sialia sialis

The Eastern Bluebird is a small thrush with vibrant blue plumage and a reddish-orange chest. It prefers open woodlands and is often seen perched on wires hunting insects.

Scientific NameSialia sialis
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatOpen woodland and farmland
DietOmnivore — insects and berries
Weight27-32 g
Size16–21 cm body length
Speed40 km/h (flight)
Lifespan6-10 years
RangeEastern North America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Eastern Bluebirds nest in cavities and readily use nest boxes.
Their diet mainly consists of insects and berries.
Males are brighter colored than females to attract mates.
They were once in decline due to habitat loss but have recovered.
