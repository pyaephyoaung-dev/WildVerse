Mountain Gorilla

Gorilla beringei beringei

Mountain gorillas live in the high-altitude forests of Central Africa and share 98% of their DNA with humans. Dedicated conservation has increased their population to over 1,000 individuals.

Scientific NameGorilla beringei beringei
Class / OrderMammalia, Primates
HabitatMontane Rainforests
DietHerbivore — leaves, stems, fruit
Weight100–200 kg
Size1.4–1.8 m tall
SpeedSlow-moving, up to 25 km/h short bursts
Lifespan35–40 years
RangeDRC, Uganda & Rwanda
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~1,000 individuals

Interesting Facts

Mountain gorilla populations have been growing thanks to ecotourism and conservation.
They spend most of their day eating — up to 34 kg of vegetation daily.
Silverback males communicate with chest beats heard from 1.6 km away.
They have been observed making tools, comforting each other, and even laughing.
