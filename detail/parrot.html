African Grey Parrot

Psittacus erithacus

The African grey parrot is widely regarded as one of the most intelligent bird species. Capable of mimicking human speech with impressive accuracy, they have the cognitive ability of a 5-year-old child.

Scientific NamePsittacus erithacus
Class / OrderAves, Psittaciformes
HabitatDense Tropical Forests
DietOmnivore — seeds, nuts, fruits
Weight400–600 g
Size33 cm body length
SpeedUp to 40 km/h
Lifespan40–60 years
RangeWest & Central Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationVulnerable, unknown exact numbers

Interesting Facts

Research subject Alex learned over 100 words and could identify objects, shapes, and colours.
African greys can understand the concept of zero — a rare cognitive feat.
They often outlive their human owners when cared for in captivity.
Wild populations have declined over 90% due to trapping for the pet trade.
