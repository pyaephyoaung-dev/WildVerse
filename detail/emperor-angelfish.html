Emperor Angelfish

Pomacanthus imperator

A brightly colored reef fish, the emperor angelfish displays striking blue and yellow stripes and a dark mask over its eyes, making it one of the most recognizable species in coral reefs.

Scientific NamePomacanthus imperator
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Perciformes
HabitatCoral reefs
DietOmnivore — sponges, algae, small invertebrates
WeightUp to 2 kg
SizeUp to 40 cm length
SpeedModerate swimmer
LifespanUp to 20 years
RangeIndo-Pacific region
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Juveniles have a different coloration than adults.
Feeds mainly on sponges and algae.
Territorial and solitary except during breeding.
Plays a role in reef ecology by controlling sponge growth.
