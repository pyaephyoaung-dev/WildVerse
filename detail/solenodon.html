Solenodon

Solenodon paradoxus

The solenodon is a nocturnal insectivorous mammal with venomous saliva, one of the few venomous mammals. It has a long flexible snout and lives in burrows. Solenodons are considered living fossils due to their primitive traits.

Interesting Facts

Solenodons use venom to subdue prey.
They have poor eyesight but excellent smell and hearing.
Their ancestors date back 76 million years.
They have a low reproductive rate with one or two offspring.
