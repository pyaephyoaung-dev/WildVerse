Western Honey Bee

Apis mellifera

The Western Honey Bee is a vital pollinator species known for producing honey and wax. It lives in complex eusocial colonies with a single queen.

Scientific NameApis mellifera
Class / OrderInsecta, Hymenoptera
HabitatVarious including meadows, forests, and urban areas
DietNectar and pollen
Weight80-100 mg
Size1.2–1.5 cm length
Speed24 km/h flying speed
Lifespan5-6 weeks (worker), 2-5 years (queen)
RangeNative to Europe, Asia; introduced worldwide
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationBillions worldwide

Interesting Facts

Uses waggle dance to communicate food locations.
Colonies can contain up to 60,000 individuals.
Crucial for pollinating many crops.
Can sting once and die afterward.
