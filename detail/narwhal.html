Narwhal

Monodon monoceros

The narwhal is a medium-sized whale known for the long, spiral tusk found on males. It is near threatened due to climate change and hunting in Arctic waters.

Scientific NameMonodon monoceros
Class / OrderMammalia, Cetacea
HabitatArctic marine waters
DietCarnivore — fish, squid, shrimp
Weight800-1,600 kg
Size4–5.5 m length
SpeedUp to 35 km/h
Lifespan50 years
RangeArctic Ocean and adjacent seas
Conservation StatusNT — Near Threatened
Population~80,000 individuals

Interesting Facts

Tusk is an elongated canine tooth, mostly in males.
Feeds primarily on fish, squid, and shrimp.
Uses echolocation to navigate icy waters.
Vulnerable to habitat changes from melting sea ice.
