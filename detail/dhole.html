Dhole

Cuon alpinus

The dhole is a highly social wild dog species known for its complex vocalizations and cooperative hunting. It is an apex predator in its ecosystem and hunts medium to large prey. Dholes have a distinctive reddish coat and a bushy tail.

Scientific NameCuon alpinus
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatForests and grasslands
DietCarnivore — medium to large ungulates
Weight10-21 kg
Size80–110 cm body length
Speed60 km/h
Lifespan10-14 years
RangeSouth and Southeast Asia
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~2,500–3,000 mature individuals

Interesting Facts

Dholes hunt in packs of up to 12 members.
Their whistle-like calls help coordinate hunts.
They can run at speeds up to 60 km/h.
Dholes have fewer toes on their hind feet than most canids.
