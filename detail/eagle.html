Bald Eagle

Haliaeetus leucocephalus

The bald eagle is the national bird of the United States. With a wingspan of up to 2.5 metres, these apex predators soar over vast territories near lakes and rivers.

Scientific NameHaliaeetus leucocephalus
Class / OrderAves, Accipitriformes
HabitatNear Large Open Bodies of Water
DietCarnivore — fish, small mammals, carrion
Weight3–6 kg
Size70–102 cm body length
SpeedUp to 120 km/h diving
Lifespan20 years
RangeNorth America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~70,000 in North America

Interesting Facts

Bald eagles can spot fish from up to 1.5 km away.
Their nests (eyries) can weigh up to 1 tonne after years of additions.
They were nearly extinct in the 1970s — conservation efforts saved them.
Bald eagles mate for life and perform breathtaking aerial courtship displays.
