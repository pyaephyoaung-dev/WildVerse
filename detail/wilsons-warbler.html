Wilson's Warbler

Cardellina pusilla

Wilson's Warbler is a small, insectivorous songbird with a bright yellow body and a distinctive black cap. It breeds in North American forests and migrates to Central America for winter.

Scientific NameCardellina pusilla
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatDeciduous and mixed woodlands
DietInsectivore — small insects and spiders
Weight6-9 g
Size10–12 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan3-7 years
RangeNorth America and Central America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Wilson's Warblers forage actively among shrubs and trees.
Males sing to defend territories during breeding season.
They build cup-shaped nests low in dense vegetation.
Their populations are stable but susceptible to habitat changes.
