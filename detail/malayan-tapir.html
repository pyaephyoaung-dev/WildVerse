Malayan Tapir

Tapirus indicus

The Malayan tapir is a large herbivorous mammal recognizable by its black and white coloration. It is endangered due to habitat loss from logging and agricultural expansion.

Scientific NameTapirus indicus
Class / OrderMammalia, Perissodactyla
HabitatTropical rainforests
DietHerbivore — leaves, twigs, fruits
Weight250-540 kg
Size1.8–2.5 m length
SpeedUp to 30 km/h
Lifespan25-30 years
RangeSoutheast Asia
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~2,500–3,000

Interesting Facts

Largest of the tapir species.
Excellent swimmers and can use trunk-like nose to breathe underwater.
Nocturnal and solitary.
Important seed dispersers in rainforest ecosystems.
