Passenger Pigeon

Ectopistes migratorius

Once the most abundant bird in North America with flocks of over 3 billion, the passenger pigeon was hunted to extinction within 50 years. The last individual, Martha, died on September 1, 1914.

Scientific NameEctopistes migratorius
Class / OrderAves, Columbiformes

Interesting Facts

Flocks were so vast they blocked out the sun for days as they passed overhead.
A single flock could number 3.5 billion birds — more than all North American birds today.
Industrial-scale hunting wiped out billions in under half a century.
Scientists are exploring cloning the passenger pigeon from museum specimens.
