Sandhill Crane

Antigone canadensis

The Sandhill Crane is a large, graceful bird with a distinctive red forehead patch. It migrates long distances and performs elaborate courtship dances during breeding season.

Scientific NameAntigone canadensis
Class / OrderAves, Gruiformes
HabitatWetlands, grasslands, and prairies
DietOmnivore — plants, grains, insects, small animals
Weight3-6 kg
Size90–122 cm body length
Speed64 km/h (flight)
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeNorth America and northeastern Siberia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~670,000 individuals

Interesting Facts

Sandhill Cranes mate for life and perform complex dances to strengthen bonds.
They communicate with loud, trumpeting calls visible over long distances.
They prefer shallow wetlands for nesting and feeding.
Populations have rebounded due to conservation efforts.
