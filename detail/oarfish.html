Oarfish

Regalecus glesne

Oarfish are the longest bony fish alive, reaching lengths up to 11 meters, and are rarely seen near the surface, often inspiring sea serpent myths.

Scientific NameRegalecus glesne
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Lampriformes
HabitatMesopelagic to epipelagic zones
DietCarnivore — small fish and plankton
WeightUp to 270 kg
SizeUp to 11 m length
SpeedSlow-moving
LifespanUp to 20 years
RangeWorldwide oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts
