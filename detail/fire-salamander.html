Fire Salamander

Salamandra salamandra

The Fire Salamander is easily recognized by its black body with vivid yellow spots or stripes. It is mostly terrestrial but breeds in water.

Scientific NameSalamandra salamandra
Class / OrderAmphibia, Caudata
HabitatDeciduous forests
DietCarnivore — insects, worms, slugs
Weight40-150 g
Size15–25 cm total length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan10-20 years
RangeCentral and Southern Europe
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Secretes toxins from skin to deter predators.
Can live up to 20 years in the wild.
Active mainly at night and after rain.
Females give birth to larvae in water.
