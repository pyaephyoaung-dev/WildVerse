Grizzly Bear

Ursus arctos

The grizzly bear is a subspecies of the brown bear and is found primarily in the northern regions of North America. They are known for their strength and adaptability. They are now listed as critically endangered.

Scientific NameUrsus arctos
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatForests & Mountains
DietOmnivore — fish, mammals, berries
Weight180–300 kg
Size1.8–2.8 m length
SpeedUp to 56 km/h
Lifespan20–30 years
RangeNorth America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~55,000 in North America

Interesting Facts

Grizzly bears are the largest land carnivores in North America.
They can live for up to 30 years in the wild.
Grizzly bears can swim upstream in rivers to catch salmon.
