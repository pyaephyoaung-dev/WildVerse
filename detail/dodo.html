Dodo

Raphus cucullatus

The dodo was a large flightless bird endemic to Mauritius. Having evolved with no natural predators, they lost their fear of humans and were hunted to extinction within 80 years of European contact.

Scientific NameRaphus cucullatus
Class / OrderAves, Columbiformes
HabitatForests of Mauritius
DietHerbivore — fruits, seeds, roots
Weight10–18 kg
SizeAbout 1 m tall
SpeedFlightless, slow-moving
LifespanVaries
RangeMauritius

Interesting Facts

The dodo became extinct just 83 years after Europeans first encountered it in 1581.
Dodos were not stupid — they simply lacked a fear of predators.
They were most closely related to the Nicobar pigeon.
Very few complete dodo skeletons exist — most museums display reconstructed specimens.
