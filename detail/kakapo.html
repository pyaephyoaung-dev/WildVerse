Kakapo

Strigops habroptilus

The kakapo is a large, nocturnal, flightless parrot endemic to New Zealand. Critically endangered, it is known for its unique booming calls and elaborate mating displays.

Scientific NameStrigops habroptilus
Class / OrderAves, Psittaciformes
HabitatTemperate forests and scrublands
DietHerbivore — seeds, fruits, leaves
Weight2-4 kg
Size58–64 cm length
SpeedFlightless; slow walker
Lifespan60-90 years
RangeNew Zealand
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
Population~250
