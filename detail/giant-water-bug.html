Giant Water Bug

Lethocerus americanus

The Giant Water Bug is a large aquatic insect known for its painful bite and predatory behavior. It uses its front legs to grasp prey such as fish and amphibians.

Scientific NameLethocerus americanus
Class / OrderInsecta, Hemiptera
HabitatFreshwater ponds and slow streams
DietCarnivore — aquatic insects, small fish, amphibians
Weight5-10 g
Size4–12 cm length
SpeedSlow swimmer
Lifespan1 year
RangeNorth and Central America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Can deliver a painful bite to humans.
Air trapped under wings allows underwater breathing.
Females lay eggs on males' backs for protection.
Also called 'toe-biters' in some regions.
