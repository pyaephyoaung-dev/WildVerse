Varied Thrush

Ixoreus naevius

The Varied Thrush is a striking songbird with a bold orange and black pattern found in coniferous forests of the Pacific Northwest. It sings haunting melodies during breeding season.

Scientific NameIxoreus naevius
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatTemperate coniferous forests
DietOmnivore — insects, berries

Interesting Facts

Varied Thrushes form monogamous pairs during breeding.
They feed on insects, berries, and seeds.
Their song is a low, eerie whistle often heard at dawn.
They migrate short distances to lower elevations in winter.
