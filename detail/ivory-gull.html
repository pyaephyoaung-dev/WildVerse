Ivory Gull

Pagophila eburnea

The Ivory Gull is a rare Arctic seabird characterized by its pure white plumage and thick yellow bill. It scavenges on carcasses and often follows polar bears for leftovers.

Scientific NamePagophila eburnea
Class / OrderAves, Charadriiformes
HabitatArctic sea ice and coastal areas
DietScavenger — carrion, fish, invertebrates
Weight600-800 g
Size53–60 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan15-20 years
RangeHigh Arctic regions of Canada, Greenland, and Russia
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationDecreasing, ~19,000 individuals

Interesting Facts

Ivory Gulls are specialized scavengers in the polar ecosystem.
They are highly adapted to cold environments with dense plumage.
Populations are declining due to climate change and pollution.
They often feed on seal remains left by polar bears.
