Poison Dart Frog

Dendrobates auratus

Poison dart frogs are among the most brightly coloured animals on Earth. Their vivid patterns warn predators of their extreme toxicity — a strategy called aposematism.

Scientific NameDendrobates auratus
Class / OrderAmphibia, Anura
HabitatTropical Rainforests
DietCarnivore — small insects and arthropods
Weight1–5 g
Size1.5–6 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan10–15 years
RangeCentral & South America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationVaries by species; many threatened

Interesting Facts

Their skin toxins are derived entirely from the wild insects they eat.
Indigenous people used their secretions to coat blowpipe darts for hunting.
Captive-bred frogs are not toxic — they lack the wild diet that produces toxins.
The golden poison frog contains enough toxin to kill 10 adult humans.
