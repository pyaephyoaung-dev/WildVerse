Yellow-headed Blackbird

Xanthocephalus xanthocephalus

The Yellow-headed Blackbird is a striking wetland bird with a bright yellow head and chest contrasting its black body. It breeds in marshes and is known for its loud, harsh calls.

Scientific NameXanthocephalus xanthocephalus
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatFreshwater marshes and wetlands
DietOmnivore — mainly insects and seeds
Weight90-115 g
Size22–28 cm length
SpeedModerate flyer, exact speed not well documented
Lifespan6-10 years
RangeWestern and central North America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~10 million

Interesting Facts

Males display by puffing feathers and singing loudly.
They nest in dense reed beds to avoid predators.
Their diet consists mainly of seeds and insects.
They often form large flocks outside breeding season.
