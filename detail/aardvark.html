Aardvark

Orycteropus afer

The aardvark is a nocturnal mammal known for its pig-like snout and powerful digging claws. It primarily feeds on ants and termites, using its long sticky tongue to capture prey. Despite its appearance, it is unrelated to pigs or anteaters.

Scientific NameOrycteropus afer
Class / OrderMammalia, Tubulidentata
HabitatSavannas and woodlands
DietInsectivore — primarily ants and termites
Weight40-65 kg
Size105–130 cm body length
Speed40 km/h
Lifespan10-23 years
RangeSub-Saharan Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern

Interesting Facts

Aardvarks can dig burrows up to 13 feet long.
They have a keen sense of smell to locate insect nests.
Their teeth lack enamel and continuously grow.
Aardvarks can close their nostrils to keep out dust while digging.
