Comb Jelly

Ctenophora

Comb jellies are gelatinous, translucent marine invertebrates known for their rows of cilia that refract light, producing a shimmering rainbow effect as they move.

Scientific NameCtenophora
Class / OrderTentaculata, Ctenophora
HabitatSurface to deep ocean waters
DietCarnivore — plankton
WeightUp to 5 kg
SizeVaries, typically 1–15 cm length
SpeedSlow swimmer
LifespanUp to 1 year
RangeWorldwide oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Use cilia for locomotion instead of muscles.
Some species are bioluminescent.
Carnivorous, feeding on plankton and small fish.
Lack stinging cells unlike jellyfish.
