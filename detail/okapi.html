Okapi

Okapia johnstoni

The okapi is a forest-dwelling mammal native to the Democratic Republic of Congo, related to the giraffe. It has striking zebra-like stripes on its legs and a long prehensile tongue for browsing foliage. Okapis are elusive and primarily solitary.

Scientific NameOkapia johnstoni
Class / OrderMammalia, Artiodactyla
HabitatDense tropical rainforests
DietHerbivore — leaves, buds, grasses
Weight200-350 kg
Size1.5–2 m body length
Speed56 km/h
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeCentral Africa (Congo)
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~15,000 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Okapis use their tongues for grooming and feeding.
Their ears can rotate independently to detect sounds.
They communicate with low-frequency sounds.
Okapis can run up to 50 km/h to escape threats.
