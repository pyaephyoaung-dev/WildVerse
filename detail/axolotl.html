Axolotl

Ambystoma mexicanum

The axolotl is a neotenic salamander known for its ability to regenerate limbs and retain larval features throughout its life. It is critically endangered due to habitat loss and pollution in its native lakes.

Scientific NameAmbystoma mexicanum
Class / OrderAmphibia, Urodela
HabitatFreshwater lakes
DietCarnivore — aquatic invertebrates, small fish
Weight0.1-0.3 kg
Size15–45 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan10-15 years
RangeMexican lakes and canals
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationCritically endangered in the wild; wild population very low

Interesting Facts

Capable of regenerating entire limbs and organs.

Native to the Xochimilco lake complex near Mexico City.
