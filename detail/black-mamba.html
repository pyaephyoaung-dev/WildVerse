Black Mamba

Dendroaspis polylepis

Africa's longest venomous snake and one of the fastest, reaching speeds of 20 km/h. Despite its name, it is grey-brown — the 'black' refers to the inside of its mouth.

Scientific NameDendroaspis polylepis
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatSavanna & Rocky Hills
DietCarnivore — birds, small mammals
Weight1.6 kg
Size2.5–4.5 m length
SpeedUp to 20 km/h
Lifespan11 years
RangeSub-Saharan Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon in range

Interesting Facts

A black mamba can deliver up to 12 strikes in a single attack.
Without antivenom, a bite is nearly 100% fatal within hours.
They are highly nervous and shy, preferring to flee rather than attack.
They can travel at speeds of up to 20 km/h for short distances.
