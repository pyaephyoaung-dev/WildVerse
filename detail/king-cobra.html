King Cobra

Ophiophagus hannah

The king cobra is the world's longest venomous snake, found in the forests of Southeast Asia. Known for their distinctive hood and potent neurotoxic venom, they are skilled hunters and prefer dense forests and grasslands.

Scientific NameOphiophagus hannah
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatTropicalForests & Grasslands
DietCarnivore — snakes and lizards
Weight180–250 kg
Size3.18–4.6 m length
SpeedUp to 20 km/h
Lifespan10–15 years
RangeIndia, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationVulnerable, unknown exact numbers

Interesting Facts

Every king cobra has unique hood patterns.
King cobras are excellent climbers.
They can run up to 20 km/h in short bursts.
A single king cobra can consume up to 1.5 kg of prey in one meal.
