Vaquita

Phocoena sinus

The vaquita is the world's rarest and smallest porpoise species, critically endangered due to bycatch in fishing nets. It inhabits shallow waters of the northern Gulf of California. Vaquitas have a distinctive facial coloration and shy behavior.

Scientific NamePhocoena sinus
Class / OrderMammalia, Cetacea
HabitatShallow coastal waters
DietCarnivore — fish and squid
Weight40-55 kg
Size1.5 m length
SpeedUp to 55 km/h
Lifespan20 years
RangeNorthern Gulf of California, Mexico
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationCritically endangered; ~10 remaining

Interesting Facts

Vaquitas have fewer than 20 individuals estimated in the wild.
They use echolocation to navigate murky waters.
Vaquitas avoid boats and human activity.
They are vulnerable due to gillnet fishing.
