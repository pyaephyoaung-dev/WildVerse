Anglerfish

Lophiiformes

Anglerfish are deep-sea predators known for their bioluminescent lure used to attract prey in the dark ocean depths. They have large mouths with sharp teeth and can swallow prey larger than themselves.

Scientific NameLophiiformes
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Lophiiformes
HabitatDeep-sea ocean
DietCarnivore — fish and invertebrates
WeightUp to 6 kg
SizeUp to 1.2 m length depending on species
SpeedSlow swimmer
LifespanUp to 20 years
RangeWorldwide in deep ocean waters
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Females are significantly larger than males.
The bioluminescent lure is a modified dorsal spine.
Some species exhibit sexual parasitism where males fuse to females.
They can survive extreme pressures of the deep ocean.
