Aepyornis

Aepyornis maximus

The Aepyornis, or elephant bird, was a giant flightless bird native to Madagascar. It was among the heaviest birds to have ever lived and went extinct due to human activity around the 17th century.

Scientific NameAepyornis maximus
Class / OrderAves, Aepyornithiformes

Interesting Facts

Aepyornis eggs were the largest of any bird, some exceeding 13 inches in length.
It could not fly due to its massive size and reduced wings.
Extinction is believed to be caused by hunting and habitat destruction by humans.
Fossils were first described scientifically in the 19th century.
