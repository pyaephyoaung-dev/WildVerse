Nile Monitor

Varanus niloticus

The Nile Monitor is a large, agile lizard native to Africa known for its swimming ability and carnivorous diet. It inhabits diverse environments from rivers to savannas.

Scientific NameVaranus niloticus
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatRivers, lakes, and savannas
DietCarnivore — fish, amphibians, small mammals, birds
Weight3-8 kg
Size1.0–2.1 m total length
SpeedUp to 30 km/h
Lifespan8-12 years
RangeSub-Saharan Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Nile Monitors are excellent swimmers and climbers.
They have sharp claws and powerful jaws.
Their diet includes fish, birds, and small mammals.
They lay large clutches of eggs in burrows.
