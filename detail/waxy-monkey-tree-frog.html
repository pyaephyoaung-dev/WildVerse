Waxy Monkey Tree Frog

Phyllomedusa sauvagii

This South American tree frog secretes a waxy coating to prevent water loss in arid environments. It is known for climbing using opposable fingers and toes.

Scientific NamePhyllomedusa sauvagii
Class / OrderAmphibia, Anura
HabitatDry forests and savannas
DietCarnivore — insects and small invertebrates
Weight15-30 g
Size7–8 cm body length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan8-12 years
RangeArgentina, Bolivia, Paraguay
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown
