Alpine Newt

Ichthyosaura alpestris

The Alpine Newt is a brightly colored salamander found in mountainous regions. It is known for its vibrant blue belly and orange throat during breeding season.

Scientific NameIchthyosaura alpestris
Class / OrderAmphibia, Caudata
HabitatMountain forests and ponds
DietCarnivore — aquatic invertebrates, small amphibians
Weight15-30 g
Size9–12 cm total length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan8-15 years
RangeCentral and Southern Europe
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Males develop bright coloration to attract females.
They hibernate during winter in moist soil.
They feed mainly on small invertebrates.
Can regenerate lost limbs and tails.
