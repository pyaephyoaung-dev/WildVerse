Blue-Ringed Octopus

Hapalochlaena spp.

Small and brightly colored, the blue-ringed octopus is famous for its venomous bite, which can be fatal to humans despite its small size of just a few centimeters.

Scientific NameHapalochlaena spp.
Class / OrderCephalopoda, Octopoda
HabitatShallow coral reefs and tide pools
DietCarnivore — small crabs, shrimp, fish
WeightUp to 30 grams
Size12–20 cm arm span
SpeedModerate swimmer
Lifespan2 years
RangePacific and Indian Oceans near Australia and Japan
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Produces tetrodotoxin, a powerful neurotoxin.
Displays bright blue rings when threatened.
Smallest octopus species with deadly venom.
Feeds mainly on crustaceans and small fish.
