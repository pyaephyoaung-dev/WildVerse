Cicada Killer Wasp

Sphecius speciosus

This large wasp hunts cicadas to provision its nests. Despite their intimidating size, cicada killers are generally not aggressive toward humans.

Scientific NameSphecius speciosus
Class / OrderInsecta, Hymenoptera
HabitatSandy soils in open areas
DietCarnivore — cicadas (larvae feed on cicadas), adults feed on nectar
Weight0.5-1 g
Size25–35 mm length
SpeedFlight speed up to 24 km/h
Lifespan1 year
RangeEastern United States
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon in native range

Interesting Facts

Females paralyze cicadas to feed larvae.
Males patrol territories but cannot sting.
Nests are burrows dug in sandy soil.
Adults feed on nectar and plant sap.
