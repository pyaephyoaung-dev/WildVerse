Eastern Box Turtle

Terrapene carolina carolina

The Eastern Box Turtle is a terrestrial turtle known for its hinged shell that allows it to close completely. It has a varied diet and is often found in deciduous forests and meadows.

Scientific NameTerrapene carolina carolina
Class / OrderReptilia, Testudines
HabitatDeciduous forests and grasslands
DietOmnivore — insects, fruits, vegetation
Weight0.7-1.3 kg
Size12–15 cm shell length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan30-40 years
RangeEastern United States
Conservation StatusNT — Near Threatened
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

They are omnivores, eating plants, insects, and fungi.
Their hinged shell provides excellent protection.
Eastern Box Turtles have strong homing instincts.
They are slow movers but can live several decades.
