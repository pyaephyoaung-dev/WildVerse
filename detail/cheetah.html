Cheetah

Acinonyx jubatus

The cheetah is the fastest land animal on Earth, reaching 112 km/h. Unlike other big cats, cheetahs cannot roar — instead they purr, chirp, and yelp.

Scientific NameAcinonyx jubatus
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatOpen Savanna & Dry Forest
DietCarnivore — primarily small to medium ungulates
Weight40–65 kg
Size1.1–1.5 m body length
SpeedUp to 112 km/h
Lifespan10–12 years
RangeAfrica & Iran
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~7,100 in the wild

Interesting Facts

A cheetah can go from 0 to 100 km/h in just 3 seconds.
Their black tear marks reduce sun glare for better hunting vision.
Cheetahs are the only big cats that cannot fully retract their claws.
Fewer than 7,000 cheetahs remain in the wild today.
