Emperor Penguin

Aptenodytes forsteri

The Emperor penguin is the tallest and heaviest penguin species. They breed in the harsh Antarctic winter, with males incubating eggs on their feet for over two months without food.

Scientific NameAptenodytes forsteri
Class / OrderAves, Sphenisciformes
HabitatAntarctic Ice & Ocean
DietCarnivore — fish, krill, squid
Weight22–45 kg
Size1.1–1.3 m height
Speed5–10 km/h swimming
Lifespan20 years
RangeAntarctica
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~595,000 individuals

Interesting Facts

Emperor penguins can dive to 550 metres and hold their breath for 22 minutes.
Males lose up to 45% of their body weight during incubation.
They huddle in rotating groups at temperatures as low as −60°C.
Chicks recognise their parents' calls among thousands of others.
