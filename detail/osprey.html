Osprey

Pandion haliaetus

The Osprey is a large raptor specialized in catching fish, often seen hovering over water before diving. It has reversible outer toes and sharp talons adapted for gripping slippery prey.

Scientific NamePandion haliaetus
Class / OrderAves, Accipitriformes
HabitatCoastal areas, rivers, and lakes
DietCarnivore — primarily fish
Weight1.4-2 kg
Size50–66 cm body length, wingspan 127–180 cm
Speed80 km/h (flight)
Lifespan7-10 years
RangeWorldwide except Antarctica
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Ospreys build large nests reused and added to each year.
They have dense, oily feathers that repel water.
The species is a bioindicator of aquatic ecosystem health.
They can dive from heights of up to 30 meters to catch fish.
