Banded Garden Spider

Argiope trifasciata

This striking orb-weaver spider is recognized by its bold yellow and black striped abdomen. It builds large circular webs and is commonly found in gardens and fields.

Scientific NameArgiope trifasciata
Class / OrderArachnida, Araneae
HabitatGardens, fields, and forests
DietCarnivore — insects
Weight0.1-0.3 g
SizeBody length 12–25 mm
SpeedStationary web spinner; movement speed not notable
Lifespan1 year
RangeNorth and South America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon

Interesting Facts

Weaves a stabilimentum in its web that reflects UV light.
Females are significantly larger than males.
Feeds primarily on flying insects like flies and bees.
Can rebuild its web daily after damage.
