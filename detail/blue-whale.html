Blue Whale

Balaenoptera musculus

The blue whale is the largest animal ever known to exist on Earth. At up to 33 metres and 190 tonnes, its heart alone is the size of a small car.

Scientific NameBalaenoptera musculus
Class / OrderMammalia, Cetacea
HabitatOpen Ocean
DietCarnivore — krill
WeightUp to 190,000 kg
Size24–30 m length
SpeedUp to 50 km/h
Lifespan80–90 years
RangeAll Oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~10,000–25,000

Interesting Facts

A blue whale's heartbeat is so loud it can be detected from 3 km away.
Baby blue whales gain 90 kg of weight per day, drinking 400 litres of milk.
They can produce sounds louder than a jet engine — up to 188 decibels.
Their populations were reduced by 99% during the whaling era of the 20th century.
