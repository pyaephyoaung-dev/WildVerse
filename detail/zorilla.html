Zorilla

Ictonyx striatus

The zorilla, or striped polecat, is a small carnivore with bold black and white stripes and a strong musk spray for defense. It is nocturnal and feeds on rodents, insects, and birds. Native to Africa, it is known for its fearless behavior.

Scientific NameIctonyx striatus

HabitatSavannas and open forests

Interesting Facts

Zorillas can spray a foul-smelling musk up to 1 meter.
They are excellent climbers and swimmers.
Their bold coloration warns predators of their defense.
Zorillas are solitary except during mating season.
