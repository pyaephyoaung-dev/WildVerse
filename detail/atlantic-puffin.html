Atlantic Puffin

Fratercula arctica

The Atlantic Puffin is a small seabird known for its colorful beak and charming appearance. It nests in burrows along rocky coasts and is an excellent swimmer using its wings to 'fly' underwater.

Scientific NameFratercula arctica
Class / OrderAves, Charadriiformes
HabitatCoastal cliffs and islands
DietCarnivore — primarily fish and marine invertebrates
Weight400-500 g
Size28–30 cm body length
Speed55 km/h (flight)
Lifespan20-25 years
RangeNorth Atlantic Ocean, from northeastern North America to northern Europe
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
Population~12 million individuals

Interesting Facts

Its beak brightens during breeding season and dulls after.
Puffins can dive up to 60 meters to catch fish.
They use their wings to swim underwater rather than feet.
Puffins mate for life and return to the same burrow annually.
