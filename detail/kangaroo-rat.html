Kangaroo Rat

Dipodomys spp.

Kangaroo rats are small desert rodents known for their powerful hind legs and hopping locomotion. They survive without direct water intake by metabolizing seeds. Their cheek pouches store food for transport and consumption.

Scientific NameDipodomys spp.
Class / OrderMammalia, Rodentia
HabitatArid deserts and scrublands
DietGranivore — seeds and vegetation
Weight70-180 grams
Size10–15 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan2-5 years
RangeWestern North America

Interesting Facts

Kangaroo rats can leap over 2 meters in a single bound.
They have adapted kidneys to conserve water.
They communicate with foot drumming.
Their burrows help regulate temperature extremes.
