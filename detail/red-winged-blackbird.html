Red-winged Blackbird

Agelaius phoeniceus

The Red-winged Blackbird is a common North American songbird known for its distinctive red and yellow shoulder patches. Males are territorial and vocal, often seen in marshes and fields.

Scientific NameAgelaius phoeniceus
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatWetlands, marshes, and open fields
DietOmnivore — seeds, insects
Weight40-70 g
Size16–23 cm body length
SpeedN/A

Interesting Facts

Males display their red shoulder patches to attract females and deter rivals.
They are highly adaptable and thrive in various habitats.
Females build nests in dense vegetation over water.
Their diet includes seeds, insects, and small aquatic animals.
