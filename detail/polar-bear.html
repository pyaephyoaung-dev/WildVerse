Polar Bear

Ursus maritimus

The polar bear is the world's largest land carnivore. Supremely adapted for cold environments, they depend on sea ice for hunting and are threatened by climate change.

Scientific NameUrsus maritimus
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatArctic Sea Ice & Tundra
DietCarnivore — primarily seals
Weight350–700 kg
Size2.4–3 m length
SpeedUp to 40 km/h on land
Lifespan20–25 years
RangeArctic Circle
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~22,000–31,000 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Polar bears can swim at 10 km/h and up to 100 km without stopping.
Their fur appears white but is actually transparent — it scatters light.
A polar bear's liver contains enough Vitamin A to be toxic to humans.
Climate change is melting their sea ice habitat at an alarming rate.
