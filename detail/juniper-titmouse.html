Juniper Titmouse

Baeolophus ridgwayi

The Juniper Titmouse is a small gray bird with a crest, commonly found in dry woodlands dominated by juniper trees. It is active and social, often seen flitting in small groups.

Scientific NameBaeolophus ridgwayi
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatJuniper woodlands and dry forests
DietOmnivore — insects, seeds, berries
Weight11-14 g
Size11–13 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan6-8 years
RangeSouthwestern United States and northern Mexico

Interesting Facts

It often stores food in bark crevices for later use.
Juniper Titmice have a distinctive high-pitched call.
They feed primarily on insects and seeds.
This species is closely related to the Oak Titmouse.
