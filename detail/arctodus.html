Arctodus

Arctodus simus

Known as the short-faced bear, Arctodus was one of the largest terrestrial mammalian carnivores in North America during the Pleistocene. It likely was an omnivore with a powerful build adapted for fast running.

Interesting Facts

Arctodus is estimated to have stood up to 1.5 meters at the shoulder.
It had long legs that suggest it was capable of high-speed pursuit.
Fossil evidence suggests it scavenged as well as hunted.
Extinct approximately 11,000 years ago at the end of the Ice Age.
