American Alligator

Alligator mississippiensis

The American Alligator is a large crocodilian native to the southeastern United States. It plays a crucial role in wetland ecosystems by controlling prey populations and creating habitats for other species.

Scientific NameAlligator mississippiensis
Class / OrderReptilia, Crocodylia
HabitatFreshwater swamps and rivers
DietCarnivore — fish, birds, mammals
Weight360-450 kg
Size3.4–4.6 m body length
Speed35 km/h (short bursts)
Lifespan30-50 years
RangeSoutheastern United States
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~5 million

Interesting Facts

They can live up to 50 years in the wild.
American Alligators can run up to 35 mph on land in short bursts.
They have a powerful bite force of over 2,000 psi.
Alligators build nests from vegetation to incubate eggs.
