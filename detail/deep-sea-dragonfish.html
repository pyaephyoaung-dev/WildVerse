Deep Sea Dragonfish

Grammatostomias flagellibarba

This fierce deep-sea predator has a hinged skull and long fang-like teeth, with bioluminescent photophores along its body to lure prey in the abyssal zone.

Scientific NameGrammatostomias flagellibarba
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Stomiiformes
HabitatDeep ocean, 200-2,000 meters
DietCarnivore — fish and crustaceans
WeightUp to 50 grams
SizeUp to 40 cm length
SpeedSlow swimmer
LifespanUnknown, estimated 5-10 years
RangeWorldwide deep oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Can produce red bioluminescent light invisible to most prey.
Has expandable jaw to swallow large prey.
Lives in near-total darkness.
Females are larger and more aggressive than males.
