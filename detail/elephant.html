African Elephant

Loxodonta africana

The African elephant is the largest land animal on Earth. Known for their intelligence, complex emotions, and tight family bonds, they are keystone species of African ecosystems.

Scientific NameLoxodonta africana
Class / OrderMammalia, Proboscidea
HabitatForests & Savannas
DietHerbivore — grasses, leaves, bark
Weight4,000–7,500 kg
SizeUp to 3.3 m tall at shoulder
SpeedUp to 40 km/h
Lifespan60–70 years
RangeSub-Saharan Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~415,000 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Elephants can recognize themselves in a mirror — a sign of self-awareness.
They communicate using infrasonic rumbles below human hearing.
An elephant's trunk has over 40,000 muscles and can lift 300 kg.
Elephants mourn their dead and revisit the bones of deceased family members.
