Burmese Python

Python bivittatus

One of the largest snake species in the world, the Burmese Python is native to Southeast Asia. It has become an invasive species in parts of the US, impacting native wildlife.

Scientific NamePython bivittatus
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatTropical and subtropical forests, grasslands

Interesting Facts

Burmese Pythons can grow over 5 meters long.
They are excellent swimmers and can stay submerged for up to 30 minutes.
Females incubate eggs by shivering to generate heat.
Their diet includes birds, mammals, and reptiles.
