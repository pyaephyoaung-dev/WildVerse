Walking Stick

Phasmatodea order

Walking sticks are insects that mimic twigs and sticks as a form of camouflage. They feed primarily on leaves and are mostly nocturnal.

Scientific NamePhasmatodea order
Class / OrderInsecta, Phasmatodea
HabitatForests and shrublands
DietHerbivore — leaves
Weight2-10 g
Size10–30 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan1-3 years
RangeWorldwide in tropical and subtropical regions
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Can regenerate lost limbs during molting.
Some species reproduce asexually.
Use slow swaying motions to mimic plant movement.
Have strong mandibles for chewing leaves.
