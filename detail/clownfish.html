Clownfish

Amphiprioninae

Clownfish live in a remarkable symbiosis with sea anemones. A special mucus protects them from stings, and they defend the anemone from predators in return for shelter.

Scientific NameAmphiprioninae
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Perciformes
HabitatCoral Reefs
DietOmnivore — algae, plankton, small invertebrates
Weight100–250 g
Size10–18 cm length
SpeedSlow swimmer
Lifespan6–10 years
RangeIndian & Pacific Oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon in native range

Interesting Facts

All clownfish are born male — the dominant fish in a group becomes female.
They generate mucus that mimics the chemistry of the anemone.
Clownfish never stray more than a few metres from their host anemone.
They are highly territorial and will aggressively defend their home.
