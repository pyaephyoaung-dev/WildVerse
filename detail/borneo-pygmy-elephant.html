Borneo Pygmy Elephant

Elephas maximus borneensis

The Borneo pygmy elephant is a subspecies of the Asian elephant, smaller and with straighter tusks. It is endangered mainly due to habitat fragmentation and human conflict.

Scientific NameElephas maximus borneensis
Class / OrderMammalia, Proboscidea
HabitatTropical rainforests and lowland forests
DietHerbivore — leaves, bark, fruits
Weight2,000-3,000 kg
Size2–2.5 m height at shoulder
SpeedUp to 25 km/h
Lifespan60-70 years
RangeBorneo island
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~1,500–2,000

Interesting Facts

Smaller than mainland Asian elephants.
Known for high intelligence and complex social structures.
Tusks grow downward instead of outward.
Threatened by deforestation and palm oil plantations.
