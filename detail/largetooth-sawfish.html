Largetooth Sawfish

Pristis pristis

The largetooth sawfish is a ray with a long, toothed rostrum used for hunting and defense. Critically endangered, it faces threats from fishing nets and habitat degradation.

Scientific NamePristis pristis
Class / OrderChondrichthyes, Rhinopristiformes
HabitatCoastal marine and freshwater rivers
DietCarnivore — fish and crustaceans
Weight90-260 kg
SizeUp to 7 m length
SpeedSlow swimmer
Lifespan30-50 years
RangeTropical and subtropical coastal waters worldwide
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationCritically endangered; unknown but very low

Interesting Facts
