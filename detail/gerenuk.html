Gerenuk

Litocranius walleri

The gerenuk is a long-necked antelope that stands on its hind legs to browse high bushes and trees. Known as the giraffe gazelle, it has a slender body and large eyes. It survives in arid and semi-arid regions by eating leaves and shoots.

Scientific NameLitocranius walleri
Class / OrderMammalia, Artiodactyla
HabitatDry bushlands and scrub
DietHerbivore — leaves, shoots, fruits
Weight18-29 kg
Size90–105 cm body length
Speed50 km/h
Lifespan10-15 years
RangeHorn of Africa and East Africa
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern

Interesting Facts

Gerenuks can stand upright to reach foliage.
They rarely drink water, getting moisture from plants.
Males have lyre-shaped horns.
Their large eyes aid in spotting predators.
