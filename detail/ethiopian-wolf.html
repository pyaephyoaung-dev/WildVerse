Ethiopian Wolf

Canis simensis

The Ethiopian wolf is Africa's rarest canid, specialized in hunting rodents in Afro-alpine regions. It is endangered due to habitat loss and disease from domestic dogs.

Scientific NameCanis simensis
Class / OrderMammalia, Carnivora
HabitatAfro-alpine grasslands and moorlands
DietCarnivore — rodents and small mammals
Weight11-19 kg
Size60–75 cm body length
SpeedUp to 50 km/h
Lifespan8-12 years
RangeEthiopian highlands
Conservation StatusEN — Endangered
Population~500

Interesting Facts

Specialized rodent hunter with slender build.
Lives in packs but hunts alone or in pairs.
Threatened by rabies and canine distemper outbreaks.
Has distinct reddish coat with white markings.
