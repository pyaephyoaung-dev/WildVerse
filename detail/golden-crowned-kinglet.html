Golden-crowned Kinglet

Regulus satrapa

The Golden-crowned Kinglet is a tiny songbird with a distinctive golden patch on its head. It flits actively through coniferous forests searching for small insects and spiders.

Scientific NameRegulus satrapa
Class / OrderAves, Passeriformes
HabitatConiferous and mixed forests
DietInsectivore — small insects and spiders
Weight5-7 g
Size8–11 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan6-7 years
RangeNorth America, from Canada to northern Mexico
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

It is one of the smallest passerines in North America.
The golden crown is brighter in males and displayed during courtship.
They often join mixed-species flocks in winter.
Kinglets have a high metabolism and are constantly active.
