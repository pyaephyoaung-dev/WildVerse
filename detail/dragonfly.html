Common Dragonfly

Libellula quadrimaculata

Dragonflies are among the most ancient insects, appearing 300 million years before the first dinosaurs. They are extraordinarily skilled hunters, catching 95% of their prey attempts.

Interesting Facts

Dragonflies can fly at 50 km/h and hover, fly backwards, and sideways.
Each of their four wings can be controlled independently.
Their compound eyes cover nearly 360° and contain up to 30,000 facets.
Nymphs live underwater for up to 5 years before emerging as adults.
