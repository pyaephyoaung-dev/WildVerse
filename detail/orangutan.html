Bornean Orangutan

Pongo pygmaeus

The Bornean orangutan shares 97% of its DNA with humans. These highly intelligent apes are found only on the island of Borneo, threatened by deforestation and hunting.

Scientific NamePongo pygmaeus
Class / OrderMammalia, Primates
HabitatTropical Rainforest
DietOmnivore — fruits, leaves, insects
Weight30–90 kg
Size1.2–1.5 m height
SpeedSlow-moving arboreal
Lifespan35–45 years
RangeBorneo
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCritically endangered, ~104,700

Interesting Facts

Orangutans are the largest tree-dwelling animals on Earth.
They use leaves as umbrellas and sticks to extract insects from bark.
Young orangutans stay with their mothers for up to 8 years.
Orangutan means 'person of the forest' in Malay.
