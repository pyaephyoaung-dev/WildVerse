Yellow Garden Spider

Argiope aurantia

This large orb-weaver spider is easily recognized by its bright yellow and black markings. It spins large webs in gardens and fields to catch flying insects.

Scientific NameArgiope aurantia
Class / OrderArachnida, Araneae
HabitatGardens, fields, and forest edges
DietCarnivore — insects caught in web
Weight0.5-1 g
Size1.5–2.5 cm body length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan1 year
RangeNorth and Central America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon

Interesting Facts

Webs often contain a stabilimentum, a zigzag silk pattern.
Females are larger and more colorful than males.
Feeds on flies, bees, and other flying insects.
Can rebuild web every day.
