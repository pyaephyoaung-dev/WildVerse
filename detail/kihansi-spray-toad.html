Kihansi Spray Toad

Nectophrynoides asperginis

This tiny toad species was discovered in the spray zones of waterfalls in Tanzania. It is critically endangered due to habitat loss and was once considered extinct in the wild.

Scientific NameNectophrynoides asperginis
Class / OrderAmphibia, Anura
HabitatWaterfall spray zones
DietCarnivore — small insects
Weight1-2 g
Size2.5–3 cm snout-vent length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan5-8 years
RangeTanzania, Kihansi Gorge
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
PopulationExtinct in the wild; captive populations exist

Interesting Facts

Gives birth to fully formed toadlets, not tadpoles.
Highly specialized to live in moist spray habitats.
Captive breeding programs exist to preserve species.
Habitat was altered by hydroelectric dam construction.
