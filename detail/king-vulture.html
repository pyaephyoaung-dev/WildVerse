King Vulture

Sarcoramphus papa

The King Vulture is a large scavenger with striking white plumage and a colorful, bald head. It plays a key role in tropical ecosystems by cleaning carcasses and preventing disease spread.

Scientific NameSarcoramphus papa
Class / OrderAves, Accipitriformes
HabitatTropical lowland forests
DietScavenger — carrion
Weight3-4 kg
Size71–81 cm body length, wingspan 1.2–2 m
SpeedN/A
Lifespan20-30 years
RangeCentral and South America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable but localized

Interesting Facts

Its colorful head coloration is unique among vultures.
It often dominates smaller vultures at carcasses.
King Vultures have a strong sense of smell to locate food.
They mate for life and build nests on the ground or cliffs.
