Veiled Chameleon

Chamaeleo calyptratus

Chameleons are famous for their ability to change colour for communication and camouflage. Their eyes can rotate independently, giving a full 360° field of view.

Interesting Facts

Chameleons change colour through layers of specialized cells called chromatophores.
Their tongue is twice their body length and strikes in under 1/100th of a second.
Each eye can rotate and focus independently, like two separate cameras.
They walk with a rocking motion to mimic leaves swaying in a breeze.
