Spiny Dogfish

Squalus acanthias

The spiny dogfish is a small shark species notable for its sharp dorsal spines and slow reproductive rate; it is widespread and adaptable to various marine environments.

Scientific NameSqualus acanthias
Class / OrderChondrichthyes, Squaliformes
HabitatCoastal and continental shelf waters
DietCarnivore — fish and invertebrates
WeightUp to 9 kg
SizeUp to 1.2 m length
SpeedUp to 40 km/h
LifespanUp to 40 years
RangeTemperate and subarctic oceans worldwide
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Has venomous spines used for defense.
