Jackson's Chameleon

Trioceros jacksonii

Jackson's Chameleon is notable for its three horn-like protrusions on the males' heads. Native to East Africa, it is an arboreal species with impressive color-changing abilities.

Scientific NameTrioceros jacksonii
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatForests and woodlands
DietInsectivore — primarily insects
Weight45-70 g
Size20–30 cm total length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan5-10 years
RangeKenya and Tanzania
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Males have three horns used in dominance displays.
They can rotate their eyes independently.
Their tongue can extend twice their body length.
Jackson's Chameleons are ovoviviparous.
