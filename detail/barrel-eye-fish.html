Barrel Eye Fish

Rhynchohyalus natalensis

The barrel eye fish is a deep-sea fish characterized by its transparent dome-shaped head housing tubular eyes that point upwards to detect prey silhouettes above.

Scientific NameRhynchohyalus natalensis
Class / OrderActinopterygii, Opisthoproctidae
HabitatMesopelagic to bathypelagic zones
DietCarnivore — small fish and plankton
WeightApproximately 1 kg
SizeUp to 15 cm length
SpeedSlow swimmer
LifespanUnknown, estimated 5-10 years
RangeTropical and subtropical Atlantic and Pacific Oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Its eyes can rotate within the transparent shield.
Head dome is filled with fluid to protect eyes.
Lives at depths between 600-1,200 meters.
Has a protrusible jaw for capturing prey.
