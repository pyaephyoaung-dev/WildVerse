Eastern Newt

Notophthalmus viridescens

The Eastern Newt is a common North American salamander known for its bright red eft stage and aquatic adult form. It has a complex life cycle involving terrestrial and aquatic habitats.

Scientific NameNotophthalmus viridescens
Class / OrderAmphibia, Caudata
HabitatPonds, lakes, and moist forests
DietCarnivore — aquatic invertebrates
Weight5-10 g
Size7–11 cm total length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan12-15 years
RangeEastern United States and Canada
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Juveniles are called red efts and are brightly colored.
Adults live mostly underwater and have smooth skin.
Produces toxins to deter predators.
Can regenerate limbs and tails.
