Red-eyed Tree Frog

Agalychnis callidryas

The red-eyed tree frog is one of the most recognizable amphibians in the world. Their bright red eyes are a defensive adaptation that can temporarily confuse predators.

Scientific NameAgalychnis callidryas
Class / OrderAmphibia, Anura
HabitatLowland Tropical Rainforests
DietCarnivore — insects
Weight6–15 g
Size3–7 cm body length
SpeedJumping, up to 1.5 m
Lifespan5 years
RangeCentral America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Their adhesive toe pads allow them to cling to vertical surfaces and even glass.
They lay eggs on leaves overhanging water so tadpoles drop directly in.
Red eyes are a 'startle coloration' strategy to confuse predators.
They are entirely nocturnal, resting on the underside of leaves by day.
