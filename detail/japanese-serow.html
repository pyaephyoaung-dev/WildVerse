Japanese Serow

Capricornis crispus

The Japanese serow is a goat-antelope native to Japan's mountainous forests. It is a solitary herbivore known for its agility on rocky terrain. This species is a cultural symbol and protected national monument in Japan.

Scientific NameCapricornis crispus
Class / OrderMammalia, Artiodactyla

Interesting Facts

Japanese serows have dense woolly fur for cold climates.
They mark territory with scent glands.
Their hooves are adapted for steep slopes.
They are active during early morning and evening.
