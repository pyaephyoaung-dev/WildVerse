Giant Isopod

Bathynomus giganteus

Giant isopods are large crustaceans resembling oversized pillbugs, scavenging the deep-sea floor mainly on the remains of dead animals.

Scientific NameBathynomus giganteus
Class / OrderMalacostraca, Isopoda
HabitatDeep sea benthic zones
DietCarnivore — scavenges dead animals and slow-moving prey
WeightUp to 1.7 kg
SizeUp to 30 cm body length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan5-10 years
RangeAtlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Can survive long periods without food.
Has a tough, segmented exoskeleton.
Lives at depths of 170 to 2,140 meters.
Slow metabolism adapted to scarce food.
