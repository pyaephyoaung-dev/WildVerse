Atlas Moth

Attacus atlas

The Atlas Moth is one of the largest moths in the world, known for its impressive wingspan and vibrant patterns. Its wings resemble snake heads, which is believed to deter predators.

Scientific NameAttacus atlas
Class / OrderInsecta, Lepidoptera
HabitatTropical and subtropical forests
DietAdults do not feed; larvae feed on leaves
Weight20-30 g
SizeWingspan up to 25–30 cm
SpeedFlight capable; speed not well documented
Lifespan1-2 weeks
RangeSoutheast Asia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationCommon in native range

Interesting Facts

Wingspan can reach up to 25 cm.
Adults do not have functional mouthparts and do not feed.
Named after the Atlas, a Titan in Greek mythology.
Caterpillars feed on cinnamon, citrus, and guava leaves.
