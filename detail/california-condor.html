California Condor

Gymnogyps californianus

The California Condor is one of the largest flying birds in North America with an impressive wingspan. Critically endangered, it has been the focus of extensive conservation efforts.

Scientific NameGymnogyps californianus
Class / OrderAves, Accipitriformes
HabitatRocky shrubland and coniferous forests
DietScavenger — carrion
Weight8-14 kg
Size100–120 cm body length, wingspan up to 3 m
Speed90 km/h (flight)
Lifespan60 years
RangeCalifornia, Arizona, Utah (USA)
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
Population~500 individuals (wild and captive)

Interesting Facts

It can soar for hours using thermal updrafts to conserve energy.
Condors have a bald head to stay clean while feeding on carrion.
They were nearly extinct with only 27 individuals in the 1980s.
Captive breeding has helped increase their wild population.
