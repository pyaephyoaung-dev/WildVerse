Water Monitor

Varanus salvator

The Water Monitor is a large lizard native to South and Southeast Asia. It is semi-aquatic and one of the most widespread monitor species.

Scientific NameVaranus salvator
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatRivers, wetlands, and mangroves
DietCarnivore — fish, amphibians, birds, carrion
Weight7-20 kg
Size1.5–2.5 m total length
SpeedUp to 30 km/h
Lifespan10-20 years
RangeSouth and Southeast Asia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Water Monitors are excellent swimmers.
They are opportunistic carnivores.
They can grow up to 3 meters in length.
They have a strong sense of smell.
