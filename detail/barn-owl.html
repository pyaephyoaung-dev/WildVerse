Barn Owl

Tyto alba

The Barn Owl is a nocturnal bird known for its heart-shaped face and exceptional night vision. It hunts small mammals silently, making it an efficient predator across many habitats.

Scientific NameTyto alba
Class / OrderAves, Strigiformes
HabitatOpen countryside, farmlands, and barns
DietCarnivore — small mammals, insects
Weight400-700 g
Size33–39 cm body length
Speed80 km/h (flight)
Lifespan4-10 years
RangeWorldwide, except polar and desert regions
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable, widespread

Interesting Facts

Barn Owls have asymmetrical ears for precise sound localization.
They swallow prey whole and regurgitate pellets of indigestible parts.
Their silent flight is due to specialized feather structure.
They have a wingspan up to 110 cm despite their moderate size.
