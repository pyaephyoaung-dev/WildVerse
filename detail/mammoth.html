Woolly Mammoth

Mammuthus primigenius

The woolly mammoth was a giant Ice Age elephant covered in thick fur, with enormous curved tusks. They roamed Eurasia and North America until around 4,000 years ago.

Scientific NameMammuthus primigenius
Class / OrderMammalia, Proboscidea
HabitatCold Tundra Steppes
DietHerbivore — grasses, shrubs
Weight4,000–6,000 kg
SizeUp to 3.5 m tall at shoulder
SpeedEstimated up to 40 km/h

Interesting Facts

Woolly mammoths coexisted with early humans, who hunted and depicted them in cave art.
Scientists have recovered intact DNA, fuelling de-extinction research.
Their tusks could grow up to 4.2 metres and curved dramatically.
A dwarf population survived on Wrangel Island until 4,000 years ago — after the pyramids.
