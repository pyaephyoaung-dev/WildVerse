Komodo Dragon

Varanus komodoensis

The Komodo dragon is the world's largest living lizard, found on a few Indonesian islands. They are powerful predators with serrated teeth and venom glands that prevent blood clotting.

Scientific NameVaranus komodoensis
Class / OrderReptilia, Squamata
HabitatTropical Dry Forests
DietCarnivore — large mammals, carrion
WeightUp to 70 kg
Size2–3 m total length
SpeedUp to 20 km/h
Lifespan30 years
RangeKomodo Islands, Indonesia
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
Population~3,000–5,000 in the wild

Interesting Facts

Komodo dragons can detect carrion from up to 9.5 km away.
They can eat up to 80% of their body weight in a single feeding.
Female Komodo dragons can reproduce without males through parthenogenesis.
Their saliva contains over 50 strains of bacteria as a secondary attack mechanism.
