Titicaca Water Frog

Telmatobius culeus

An aquatic frog endemic to Lake Titicaca, it has loose, baggy skin that increases oxygen absorption in low-oxygen water. It is critically endangered due to overharvesting and pollution.

Scientific NameTelmatobius culeus
Class / OrderAmphibia, Anura
HabitatHigh-altitude freshwater lakes
DietCarnivore — aquatic invertebrates
Weight150-300 g
SizeUp to 26 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving aquatic
LifespanUnknown
RangeLake Titicaca, Peru and Bolivia
Conservation StatusCR — Critically Endangered
