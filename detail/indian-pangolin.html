Indian Pangolin

Manis crassicaudata

The Indian pangolin is a scaled mammal that feeds primarily on ants and termites. Its thick keratin scales serve as armor against predators. It is nocturnal and uses its strong claws to dig into insect nests.

Scientific NameManis crassicaudata
Class / OrderMammalia, Pholidota
HabitatForests and grasslands
DietInsectivore — ants and termites
Weight8-14 kg
Size60–90 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan10-17 years
RangeIndian subcontinent
Conservation StatusVU — Vulnerable
PopulationUnknown; vulnerable

Interesting Facts

Indian pangolins can roll into a tight ball for defense.
Their scales are made of keratin, like human nails.
They have no teeth and swallow small stones to aid digestion.
Pangolins are heavily trafficked for scales and meat.
