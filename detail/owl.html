Great Horned Owl

Bubo virginianus

The great horned owl is one of North America's most powerful and adaptable predators, capable of taking prey much larger than itself.

Scientific NameBubo virginianus
Class / OrderAves, Strigiformes
HabitatForests, Deserts & Urban Areas
DietCarnivore — mammals, birds, reptiles
Weight1–2.5 kg
Size45–63 cm body length
SpeedUp to 50 km/h
Lifespan13 years
RangeAmericas
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationStable

Interesting Facts

Great horned owls can rotate their heads 270 degrees.
They regurgitate bones and fur as compact pellets.
Their talons require 28 kg of force to open — like a steel vice grip.
They are the only animal that regularly eats skunks.
