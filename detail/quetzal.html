Quetzal

Pharomachrus mocinno

The Resplendent Quetzal is famous for its brilliant green and red plumage and long tail feathers. It inhabits cloud forests and holds cultural significance in Central America.

Scientific NamePharomachrus mocinno
Class / OrderAves, Trogoniformes
HabitatMontane cloud forests
DietOmnivore — fruits, insects, small animals
Weight210-230 g
Size32–36 cm body length
SpeedN/A
Lifespan20 years

Interesting Facts

Quetzals feed mainly on fruits, especially wild avocados.
They are elusive and difficult to spot in dense forests.
Tail feathers of males can reach up to a meter long.
They were considered sacred by ancient Mesoamerican cultures.
