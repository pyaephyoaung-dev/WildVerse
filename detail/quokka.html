Quokka

Setonix brachyurus

The quokka is a small marsupial native to southwestern Australia, famous for its friendly nature and 'smiling' appearance. It is nocturnal and herbivorous, feeding mainly on grasses and leaves. Quokkas are excellent climbers and swimmers.

Interesting Facts

Quokkas can survive long periods without water.
They reproduce once a year with a short gestation.
Quokkas use hopping as their primary locomotion.
They have a low fear of humans.
