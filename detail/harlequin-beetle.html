Harlequin Beetle

Acrocinus longimanus

This large, vividly colored beetle is notable for its long forelegs and striking red, black, and yellow markings. It is a wood-boring species found in tropical forests.

Scientific NameAcrocinus longimanus
Class / OrderInsecta, Coleoptera
HabitatTropical rainforests
DietFeeds on wood and decaying plant matter
Weight10-15 g
Size4–7 cm length
SpeedSlow-moving
Lifespan1-2 years
RangeCentral and South America
Conservation StatusLC — Least Concern
PopulationUnknown

Interesting Facts

Males have forelegs longer than their bodies.
Larvae develop in decaying wood.
Adults feed on sap and fruit.
Name derives from colorful pattern resembling harlequin costume.
